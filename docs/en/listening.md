## Common Mistakes in English Listening Practice

### 1. Scattered Learning Materials

Subscribing to too much listening content—VOA, BBC, various Podcasts, English classic readings—leads to an inability to stably allocate time every day to listen.

**Suggestion**: Choose what interests you most, evaluate your learning time, and subscribe to an appropriate amount of content.

### 2. Materials Beyond Your Level

Many people try VOA from the beginning, which is not recommended. Even VOA slow English, you will encounter too many new words that cause you to get stuck and lose interest in continuing.

**Suggestion**: Choose listening materials that match your vocabulary level, step by step, gradually.

### 3. Over-reliance on Subtitles

Students who like watching American TV shows may develop an illusion—watching with bilingual subtitles while listening, mistakenly thinking they can understand most of the content.

**Suggestion**: When you re-watch a favorite show for the second or third time, try turning off subtitles. Relying on previously memorized content will help improve your listening.

### 4. Choosing Uninteresting Materials

Even the best listening materials become a chore if you have no interest in them.

### 5. Unconscious Listening

We spend a lot of time listening to English songs, but few people consciously listen to the lyrics' content, wasting many opportunities to improve listening.

## Intensive Listening vs. Extensive Listening

### Intensive Listening

First understand the listening material, grasp the meaning of the expression, then try to hear every word clearly. Intensive listening usually involves spelling or oral repetition practice.

**Recommended Materials**: New Concept English Book 3 and Book 4

### Extensive Listening

Extensive listening helps us naturally contact English, cultivate English language sense, and understand authentic English.

It also significantly helps improve vocabulary and speaking skills. Audiobooks, movies, TV shows, and music are all good materials for extensive listening.

## Listening Practice Tips

::: tip Practice Methods
1. **Fixed time daily**: Develop a habit, persist in practice
2. **Start simple**: Don't challenge high-difficulty materials from the start
3. **Active listening**: Consciously understand content, not passively listen
4. **Take notes**: Record new words and important expressions
5. **Repeat listening**: Good materials are worth listening to multiple times
:::

::: warning Note
Listening improvement is a gradual process. Don't expect huge progress in the short term. Be patient, keep practicing, and you'll find yourself improving without realizing it.
:::
